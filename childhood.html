Growing Up in Meru

Beautiful memories from my childhood in Meru County

Early Years in Meru (2005-2010)

I was born and raised in the beautiful Meru County, surrounded by the majestic Mount Kenya. Growing up in a close-knit community taught me the values of unity, respect, and hard work from an early age.

Some of my earliest memories include helping my grandmother in her shamba (farm), where I learned about different crops and the importance of agriculture in our community.

Primary School Days (2011-2017)

I attended Meru Primary School where I discovered my love for mathematics and science. My teachers recognized my analytical mind and encouraged me to participate in science fairs and math competitions.

During this time, I was introduced to computers for the first time in our school's computer lab. I was fascinated by how technology worked and spent hours exploring educational software.

Community Life & Traditions

Growing up in Meru meant being part of rich cultural traditions. I participated in community events, learned traditional dances, and understood the importance of "harambee" (community self-help) from a young age.

My family emphasized education while maintaining strong ties to our Meru heritage. Weekend trips to Nkunga Sacred Forest and visiting the Meru Museum were regular family activities that connected me to my roots.

Tech Curiosity Awakens

When I was 12, my uncle who worked in Nairobi brought his laptop during a visit. I was mesmerized watching him code. He showed me how to create simple HTML pages, sparking my initial interest in technology.

I started visiting cyber cafes in Meru town to learn more about computers. Despite limited resources, my curiosity drove me to learn whatever I could about technology and its potential.

Family & Meru Values

My family has been my greatest support system. As the first in my family to pursue software engineering, my parents have always encouraged my academic journey while ensuring I stay connected to our Meru values.

Weekends were often spent visiting relatives across the county, attending church together, and participating in community clean-up exercises. These experiences taught me the importance of community service and collective responsibility.

"In Meru, we believe that education is the key to unlocking potential. Your journey in technology is not just for you, but for uplifting our entire community." - My father's wisdom

Meru Values Instilled

  • Hard work & perseverance
  • Respect for elders & community
  • Education as empowerment
  • Environmental conservation
  • Cultural pride & identity
